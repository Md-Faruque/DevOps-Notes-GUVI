Email Notifications:
====================
	Email notification in Jenkins means sending an automatic email whenever a build succeeds or fails. Jenkins can send these updates to developers, testers, or operations teams so they know immediately what happened in the pipeline.

Think of it like this:
 > Without notifications → someone must log in to Jenkins and check the build status manually.
 > With notifications → Jenkins tells you directly (via email), saving time and reducing delays in fixing issues.

Why we use it in real-time
--------------------------
1. Instant Feedback → Developers know right away if their code broke something.
2. Better Collaboration → Teams (QA, DevOps, Developers) get notified at the same time.
3. Faster Fixes → If a build/test fails, developers don’t waste time; they can fix it quickly.
4. Audit Trail → Emails serve as proof of who deployed what and when.
5. Automation Advantage → No need for manual checks; Jenkins does the reporting.

In corporate projects, teams often configure email alerts so:
 . Developers get notified for failure.
 . Managers may get notified only for success or when a release is deployed.
